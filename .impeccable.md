## Design Context

### Users
Primarily UK football fans (18+), both casual ("I'll have a go") and competitive ("I track every point"). They open this on their phone on a Saturday morning before the 3pm deadline — often in a rush, checking scores from last week and making predictions for this week. They're fluent with sports betting UX, Premier League broadcast graphics, and gaming UIs. They're NOT enterprise software users — don't be boring.

### Brand personality
**Broadcast. Bold. Belonging.**
Think Premier League or Champions League production quality — not a startup SaaS. The game is free but the stakes feel real. This is a proper football game, not a casual trivia widget.

Three words: **sharp, electric, authoritative**.

### Aesthetic direction
Dark broadcast-grade gaming UI. Reference: EA FC / FIFA Ultimate Team card system — the contrast of near-black surfaces, gold/yellow accents, and club colors feels premium and familiar to the audience. NOT: generic SaaS dashboard, mobile casino/game ads, FPL clone with cards everywhere.

**Theme**: Dark. Always. Football is watched at night, on dark TVs, in pubs. The brand is built for dark.

### Typography
- **Display**: Anton — bold, condensed, uppercase authority. All headings, numbers, scoreline displays.
- **Body**: Barlow — sporty, slightly condensed, readable. UI labels, body copy, inputs. Loaded at 400/500/600/700/800.
- Both from Google Fonts.
- Scale: large contrast between display headings and body text — at least 2× ratio.
- Labels: Barlow 700, uppercase, tight letter-spacing.

### Color system (OKLCH)
- Background: `oklch(0.13 0.008 95)` — near-black with a whisper of yellow tint
- Surface: `oklch(0.19 0.007 95)` — card backgrounds
- Yellow (brand): `oklch(0.88 0.165 107)` — primary accent only (headlines, scores, CTAs)
- Text: `oklch(0.96 0.005 95)` — near-white
- Text muted: `oklch(0.68 0.008 95)` — secondary info
- Borders: `oklch(0.82 0.006 95 / 0.14)` — subtle, not dark lines
- All neutrals tinted toward the yellow hue (H=95–107).

### Key constraints
- Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS — no framework
- Hosted on Vercel, backend (Supabase) not yet built
- Must work on mobile (3pm Saturday, phone, one thumb)
- WCAG AA minimum contrast

### Anti-patterns to avoid
- Glassmorphism (backdrop-filter on everything)
- Side-stripe borders on cards (border-left > 1px — absolute ban)
- Gradient text (absolute ban)
- Dot-grid backgrounds (too much noise)
- Generic 4-card icon grids
- The same card treatment on every element
- Inter, Roboto, DM Sans, Space Grotesk (reject list fonts)

### Fixture cards
Club identity is shown via a **top border** (`border-top: 3px solid var(--club-accent)`). This is NOT a side stripe — it's a full-width top accent, like a TV match graphic header. The badge circle in the scorer row also carries the club color.

### Jackpot display
No bordered box. Just the number in huge Anton, with the yellow glow from the hero radial gradient giving it presence. Less decoration = more impact.

### Steps section
Not generic equal-height cards. Big Anton step numbers (4.5rem+) as visual anchors, with `border-top` separating steps — editorial layout, not a template.
